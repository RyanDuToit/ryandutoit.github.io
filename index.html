Ryan DuToit

Engineer, Hacker, Sci-fi Nerd

I'm a Ph.D. student at the University of Minnesota, Department of Computer Science and Engineering. I currently work as a research assistant in the MARS robotics lab [mars.cs.umn.edu]

Projects

Real-Time Pose Estimation on a Mobile Device
I have spent time designing, implementing, and testing real-time, 6DoF pose-estimation algorithms on a mobile device. Our group has contributed to the state-of-the-art in Vision-aided Inertial Navigation Systems (VINS) on mobile platforms, including a Samsung S4, Google Glass, and the Project Tango prototype. [project page link]

Cooperative Mapping
A major problem in computer-vision and robotics is map generation from visual-inertial data. I have contributed to a cooperative mapping (CM) algorithm, which fuses data collected from several users and/or robots, and generates a 3D map of point and line features. [project page link] [online map visualization]
Map-based localization
Fusing the two projects above, I have worked on leveraging a pre-built map (from CM) to enhance real-time pose estimation. Map-based localization enhances the performance of standard VINS, and allows the user to see their pose on a blueprint of the previously mapped building using only visual and inertial data. [project page link]
